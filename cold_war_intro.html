A World Divided · 1945–1991

Why "Cold"?

The war was "cold" because the two superpowers never directly fought each other in open combat. Both sides had nuclear weapons — a direct war could destroy the entire planet. Instead, they competed through proxy wars (fighting through other countries), a space race, a nuclear arms race, espionage, and propaganda.

The Iron Curtain

After WWII, the Soviet Union took control of Eastern European countries, installing communist governments. Winston Churchill described this division of Europe as an "Iron Curtain" — an invisible barrier separating the free West from the communist East. Germany itself was split: West Germany was democratic, East Germany was communist.
