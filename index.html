The Cold War

A World Divided · 1945–1991
Two Superpowers. One World.
After World War II, the US and USSR emerged as rival superpowers — but never fought each other directly.

Why "Cold"?

The war was "cold" because the two superpowers never directly fought each other in open combat. Both sides had nuclear weapons — a direct war could destroy the entire planet. Instead they competed through proxy wars, a space race, a nuclear arms race, espionage, and propaganda.

The Iron Curtain

After WWII, the Soviet Union installed communist governments across Eastern Europe. Winston Churchill described this division as an "Iron Curtain" — an invisible barrier separating the free West from the communist East. Germany itself was split in two.
